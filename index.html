Philosophy
For years, my intellectual journey has been deeply rooted in the complexities of postmodernity, analyzing the fragmentation of truth and the erosion of absolute narratives. Lately, however, my focus has shifted toward a more urgent horizon: the rapid intersection of human consciousness and machine intelligence. I am deeply concerned with the profound implications of substituting human intellect with artificial systems—a shift that challenges the very essence of our agency, creativity, and moral foundation. These reflections explore the fragile boundary between technological progress and the preservation of the human spirit in an age of automated thought.

Math
Goldbach's Conjecture
My research in number theory has recently converged on a singular, ambitious objective: the resolution of Goldbach’s Conjecture. After years of rigorous inquiry, I have developed a novel taxonomic method that categorizes even numbers into distinct groups, allowing me to approach the proof through the application of accessible mathematical tools to each group in isolation. I am pleased to share that my first paper detailing this structural approach is nearing publication. This work represents a significant step forward, demonstrating that even the most enduring mysteries in mathematics can sometimes be unraveled by reexamining their components from a fresh, simplified perspective.

Image & Signals Processing
My research in image and signal processing spans nearly four decades, a journey defined by the quest to extract clarity from complexity. Throughout my career, I have tackled diverse challenges, ranging from the development of specialized image filters for low-field magnetic resonance tomographs to the application of R/S analysis in cardiological intensive care units—a method that proved vital in saving patients' lives by identifying critical physiological patterns. This long-standing commitment to applied mathematics and engineering reflects my passion for solving real-world problems, transforming raw, noisy data into actionable insights that directly impact human health and technological capability.

Programming
Hypatia
Hypatia is a social network designed specifically for readers and academic researchers, slated for release in the second half of 2026. What began as a modest technical project to facilitate text retrieval across large masses of PDF files has evolved into a fundamentally different social ecosystem. In a landscape dominated by manipulative engagement strategies, Hypatia stands apart: it intentionally lacks an algorithm designed to push content or shape user behavior. Instead, it offers a platform where discovery is driven by genuine academic interest and intellectual pursuit, restoring agency to the community of scholars.

Sokrates
Sokrates is a comprehensive support system for educators, scheduled for launch in the second half of 2026. By leveraging the power of the internet and artificial intelligence, this platform streamlines the creation of intelligent and engaging lessons while placing a strong emphasis on the diversity of thought and cultural perspectives in every material produced. Sokrates is designed to move beyond standard pedagogical tools, encouraging a global, multifaceted approach to teaching that empowers educators to enrich their students' learning experiences with depth, inclusivity, and critical awareness.
